## Revised Universal Soil Loss Equation (RUSLE) - Background

This project leverages the **Revised Universal Soil Loss Equation (RUSLE)**, a widely recognized and empirically-derived model, to assess and predict soil erosion risk, specifically within the Tangier region of Morocco.

**What is RUSLE?**

RUSLE is a model designed to estimate the long-term average annual soil loss (often denoted as 'A') resulting from sheet and rill erosion. It is not designed for predicting erosion from concentrated flows (like gullies) but is valuable for assessing broader areas and understanding the main factors driving soil erosion.

**RUSLE Factors Explained:**

The RUSLE equation calculates soil loss (A) by multiplying five key factors that represent the major controls on soil erosion:

*   **R - Rainfall Erosivity Factor:** This factor quantifies the erosive power of rainfall and runoff. Higher R values indicate a greater potential for rainfall to cause erosion, often associated with areas of high rainfall amounts and intensities.

*   **K - Soil Erodibility Factor:** The K-factor represents the inherent susceptibility of a soil type to erosion.  Soils with high silt content, low organic matter, and poor structure generally have higher K-values and are more easily eroded.

*   **LS - Slope Length and Steepness Factor (Topographic Factor):** This factor combines the influence of slope length (L) and slope steepness (S) on erosion.  Steeper and longer slopes result in higher LS values and increased erosion potential due to greater runoff velocity and volume.

*   **C - Cover Management Factor:**  The C-factor represents the effectiveness of vegetation cover and land management practices in reducing erosion. Land with dense vegetation cover (e.g., forests) has a low C-factor, indicating good protection, while bare soil or intensively tilled agricultural land has a high C-factor, signifying little protection.

*   **P - Support Practice Factor:** This factor reflects the impact of conservation practices designed to reduce erosion, such as contour farming, terracing, or strip cropping.  Effective conservation practices result in P-factors less than 1, reducing overall soil loss.

**RUSLE Equation:**

The core of the model is the multiplicative equation:  **A = R * K * LS * C * P**

**Relevance to this Project:**

This project applies the RUSLE model within the context of Tangier, Morocco, to:

*   Identify areas prone to higher rates of soil erosion.
*   Understand the spatial distribution of erosion risk across the landscape.
*   Potentially inform land management and conservation strategies.
